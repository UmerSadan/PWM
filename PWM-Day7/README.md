# K2

K2 stands 8,611 metres (28,251 feet) on the Pakistan-China border in the Karakoram Range, the world's second-highest peak after Everest. Surveyor Thomas Montgomerie labeled it in 1856, as it never acquired a local name. Unlike Everest's gradual ridgelines, K2 forms a near-perfect pyramid, rising 3,200 metres above its glacier on the steep north side alone. Early expeditions failed for decades until Achille Compagnoni and Lino Lacedelli summited on July 31, 1954. Climber George Bell's remark after a deadly 1953 attempt gave it the name "Savage Mountain" — earned by a historic one-in-four death rate before 2021.
